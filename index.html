My mission is to help readers with data literacy

Exposure and practice is the key to learning

Data literacy is very important these days, especially with the rapidly changing priorities in today's environment.

In the past, I relied on people to interpret data for me. However, I quickly realized this is a short term fix to a long term problem.

Literacy is important, period. Teaching a man to fish is the best way to empower people to make the best decisions throughout their life.

The new generation must be able to interpret data to inform actionable decision making.

The goal of this blog is to help teach readers how to interpret data, one step at a time.

Let's start with a relevant example that most people can relate to: walkability.

I will use the Walkscore API to help users understand how to read and interpret walkability and transit scores in the area they reside in.

For this blog post, I will analyze the walkability of the city I live in, Philadelphia, which is the city of brotherly love.

Very excited to start this journey with all of you. Let's begin!

Special thanks to platforms that prioritize making data accessible for educational purposes!
